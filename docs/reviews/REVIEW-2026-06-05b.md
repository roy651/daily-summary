### Knowledge layer (net-new) — findings

The layer is well-built: never-drop routing, dedup-on-accrete, clean wiring through both pipelines,
human override survived the accumulate pass. The risks are about **what happens as it accumulates**, and
they compound with N1.

- [ ] **K1 — Accumulated `observations` + general `knowledge` are fed to the packet uncapped.** —
  **Medium** — `packet.py:64,91,138`. Evidence ids got a 25-cap (F2), but per-project/-client
  `observations` and the general store ride into every packet in full and only ever grow (dedup, never
  aged or pruned). On a long-lived project this is a second monotonic-growth source feeding the same
  packet-bloat N1 describes. *Fix:* age/size-cap what's fed (most-recent-N, or only observations newer
  than some horizon), mirroring the evidence cap; keep the full set in state.
- [ ] **K2 — Agent-inferred knowledge has no provenance/confidence tier and feeds back as established
  fact → confirmation-drift risk for the phase-2 learning loop.** — **Medium** — `apply.py:23-31`,
  `knowledge.py`, `schema.Insight`. Observations/insights are stored with `source="agent"` and replayed
  into every subsequent packet with no marker distinguishing *agent-proposed* from *human-confirmed* —
  unlike the disciplined three-way split you enforce on project status. A wrong early inference ("X is a
  subcontractor", "client Y prefers Z") becomes self-reinforcing: it's in the packet every run, reads as
  prior knowledge, and nothing prompts retraction. This is the most important *design* risk in this
  milestone because the whole point of the layer is to feed itself. *Fix:* tag observation/insight
  `source` through to the packet (so the model knows what's tentative), surface accumulated knowledge in
  `render_state_review_md` so Avigail can prune it, and reserve a human-confirmed tier for the phase-2
  consume step. Cheap now, expensive to retrofit once months of agent guesses have compounded.
- [ ] **K3 — Daily has no client-creation path; clients first seen *after* bootstrap never get a
  `ClientProfile`.** — **Medium** — `daily.run_digest` (writes `clients` unchanged; `_derive_clients`
  only runs in bootstrap). A project discovered in a daily/accumulate run for a brand-new `client_id`
  exists only as `project.client_id` with no profile — so it's absent from the state-review, has no
  `is_agency`/contacts, and a client-scoped insight for it silently falls back to the general store
  (`apply.py:180`, the `scope not in by_id` branch). Given the product runs for months and new clients
  *will* arrive, this is a real gap, not a corner case. *Fix:* derive/upsert `ClientProfile`s from
  project `client_id`s in the daily persist step too (the bootstrap helper already exists).
- [ ] **K4 — Dedup is exact-lowercased-text, so paraphrases of the same fact accrete.** — **Low** —
  `apply.py:25`, `knowledge.py:27`. "Prefers PDF proofs" and "prefers proofs as PDF" both persist; over
  many runs the store fills with restatements. Quality/bloat, not correctness — but it amplifies K1.
- [ ] **K5 — F5's new shared-evidence match can collapse two distinct *new* projects that cite the same
  thread in one run.** — **Low** — `apply.py:50-55`. If the model emits two `project_id=null` updates
  carrying a common `evidence_thread_id`, the second matches the first (now in `result`) and merges in.
  Unusual, and the safer direction would be to surface for human dedup; just flag it as a known edge of
  the otherwise-correct dedup rewrite.

### Response to N1–N3 + the pending decision

- **N1 — agree it's real; I'd refine the framing.** Packet size is bounded by the *mail volume in the
  ingest window*, not strictly unbounded: steady-state daily (~24h window) stays small. What grows is the
  **keep-rate within a window** — as the contact store saturates, a window admits closer to *all* human
  mail. So N1 bites hardest on (a) the month-wide backfill/accumulate passes and (b) long-gap catch-ups,
  and matters most for future `ApiReasoner` cost. The cheapest high-leverage lever is **tightening
  `_seed_contacts`**: today it promotes *every* human address in *every* surviving thread to a permanent
  T1 contact — including one-off senders and the N2 marketing addresses, which is the root of the
  amplification. Seed a contact only on a real signal (Avigail replied to them, or ≥2 distinct threads),
  not first inbound. That one change blunts both N1 and N2 and is low-risk. A T1 redesign / relevance-rank
  within T1 is a phase-2 cost optimization — don't block on it.
- **N2 — agree, Medium; endorse the conservative shape.** Demote only on *structural* bulk signals
  (`List-Unsubscribe`, `Precedence: bulk`, `no-reply@`, known ESP sender domains) and write what you drop
  to "Needs your eye"/a sidecar so a misfire is visible. **Do not** key off Hebrew-language detection or
  generic "newsletter" wording — high false-positive risk against real Hebrew-speaking clients, which
  violates recall-is-the-gate. With the surfaced-drops safety valve this is consistent with your invariant.
- **N3 — agree, Low; fold into the N1 fix.** When you tighten `_seed_contacts`, set an initial role from
  thread structure at the same time (someone Avigail replied to → likely client/agent; CC-only → likely
  sub/observer). Letting insights *upgrade* roles is reasonable but inherits K2's drift risk — gate it.
- **Pending decision — yes, make the *minimal* change before the April+½May backfill.** Specifically: (1)
  tighten `_seed_contacts` admission, and (2) the N2 conservative denoise with surfaced drops. Both are
  small and well-contained. Running the backfill on today's design risks a contact store you'll want to
  retroactively prune (N1) and noisy packets that make the manual GT collection in `docs/07` harder and
  costlier — both cheaper to prevent than to redo. **Defer** the deeper packet-scoping / relevance-rank /
  K1–K2 knowledge-tiering to a focused pass; they're not backfill-blocking, but K2 should land before the
  phase-2 learning loop consumes any of this.
